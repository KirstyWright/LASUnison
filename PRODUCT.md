# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

London Ambulance Service staff and the people who might join them. Four overlapping groups:

- **Front-line crews** — paramedics, EMTs, technicians.
- **Control-room staff** — Emergency Operations Centre (999) and 111 / IUC call handlers.
- **Support and corporate staff** across the service.
- **Reps, stewards and branch officers** — the people who run the branch and represent colleagues.

Context matters more than demographics here. Most visits happen **on a phone, mid-shift, with little time and often under stress** — something has gone wrong at work, or there's five minutes between jobs. The reader is rarely browsing for pleasure; they have a job to get done and want it done fast. Accessibility is part of who they are, not an add-on: tired eyes, bright cabs, gloves, small screens, intermittent signal.

## Product Purpose

The official website of the **London Ambulance Service UNISON Branch** — the UK's biggest ambulance branch, with almost 7,000 members. It exists to **represent, protect and stand up for** LAS staff, and to grow the branch.

Four jobs it does, in priority order:

1. **Get a member help, fast** — representation and the UNISON Direct phone number reachable in seconds.
2. **Recruit** — joining the union is the primary conversion.
3. **Inform** — branch news, statements and campaign updates.
4. **Explain value** — member benefits and support.

Success: a member in trouble finds help without friction, a non-member understands why the branch is worth joining and joins, and people trust the branch to tell them the truth.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, trustworthy, calm and **campaigning**. A confident public-sector voice — plain, direct, warm but serious. Three words: **solid, protective, campaigning**.

Solidarity and protection are the emotional core: the union stands with its members and stands up for them. The campaigning edge is real conviction, not noise — never corporate-slick, never playful for its own sake, never shouting. A genuine emergency register (999 red) appears only for genuinely urgent items, which is what gives it weight.

## Anti-references

What this must **not** look like:

- **Corporate / SaaS slick.** No gradient-mesh heroes, no "book a demo" energy, no glossy startup polish. This is a member union, not a product launch.
- **Dated gov template.** No grey-box GDS clone, accordion soup, or 2014-council-software feel. Public-sector does not have to mean clunky.
- **Loud / agitprop.** No all-caps shouting, clip-art megaphones, or ransom-note activism. The conviction shows in clarity and confidence, not volume — anything louder undercuts the institutional trust.

## Design Principles

1. **Help first, fast.** The most common job is "get help now." Representation and the UNISON Direct number must be reachable from anywhere in seconds, on a phone, with no hunting. Speed of access beats elegance every time.
2. **Solidarity made visible.** Protection and collective strength are the feeling to land — "7,000 members strong," real crews, concrete numbers, the branch standing with you. Show it, don't assert it.
3. **Calm authority with a campaigning edge.** Institutional and trustworthy by default; genuinely campaigning when it counts. Confident, never corporate-slick, never loud.
4. **Earn the red.** The emergency register (999 red) is reserved for the genuinely urgent. Spending it sparingly is what keeps it meaningful.
5. **Accessibility is the brief.** Designed for a tired reader, mid-shift, on a small bright screen. WCAG 2.2 AA is the floor; real-world clarity and speed are the goal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**WCAG 2.2 AA** is the committed conformance target (the UK legal standard for public-sector bodies under the Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018).

Non-negotiable, and treated as a design constraint rather than a final audit:

- Body text meets ≥4.5:1 contrast; large/UI text ≥3:1. The warm purple-tinted neutrals must be checked, not assumed.
- Full keyboard operability with a visible focus state (the design system already ships a 3px blue focus ring).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honoured everywhere; motion is calm and never required to understand content.
- Mobile-first and touch-first: large tap targets, no hover-only affordances, readable at arm's length in a moving vehicle.
- Plain language. Reference numbers, pay rates and the like set in mono so they're unambiguous.
