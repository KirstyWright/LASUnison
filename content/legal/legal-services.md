## What do UNISON legal services cover?

- **Employment advice** — expert guidance on your rights at work
- **Personal injury** — covers any accident that happens outside or in the workplace, including assaults at work, industrial disease claims, road traffic accidents and accidents on holiday
- **Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (CICA) claims**
- **Work-related criminal offences**
- **Wills** — free wills service for members, with reduced rates for family members
- **Conveyancing** — discounted rates for members and their families
- **Clinical negligence** — free screening service to assess potential legal claims for members and their family members affected by clinical negligence
- **Free legal advice on non-employment issues** from UNISON's lawyers — you are entitled to a 30-minute telephone interview

## Injuries at work — your rights explained

*Gerard Stilliard, head of personal injury claims at [Thompsons Solicitors](https://www.thompsons.law/), outlines how you can access legal support if you have been injured at work.*

The best employers recognise that proper health and safety is vital and take steps to ensure they keep their employees as safe as possible in the workplace.

At Thompsons, we see first-hand the devastating impact on workers and their families when health and safety is compromised — usually when management is under pressure to cut costs. That is why, as a proud partner of UNISON, we work with your union to actively promote the benefits of union membership and support you and your families with legal claims.

### Your employer's 'duty of care'

Your employer owes you a duty of care to protect your health, safety and welfare while at work. They should:

- Follow health and safety legislation
- Conduct adequate risk assessments
- Provide health and safety training
- Supply sufficient PPE
- Ensure equipment is in safe working order and regularly maintained
- Reduce risk as much as reasonably possible

If your employer has failed in their duty of care and you have been injured at work as a result, you may be able to bring a compensation claim.

### Making a compensation claim

The law requires employers to avoid exposing employees to foreseeable risks wherever possible. If you've been injured as a result of your employer's failure to comply with this duty, Thompsons can help you claim the compensation you deserve.

Thompsons Solicitors work on a no-win-no-fee basis for UNISON members, so you can pursue a claim without financial risk to yourself.
