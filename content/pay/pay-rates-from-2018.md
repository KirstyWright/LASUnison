#### Agenda for Change Pay Rates

View the [NHS Terms and Conditions of Service Handbook](https://www.nhsemployers.org/tchandbook) known as Agenda for Change for the full terms and conditions of NHS employment.

From 2018/19 onwards, pay points are expressed in terms of the years of experience required to attain each level of pay, measured from the anniversary of appointment. Legacy pay-point numbers are no longer used.

> **Looking for the full 2018–2021 pay scale?**
> The complete Table 10 — every band and pay point for 2018/19, 2019/20 and 2020/21 — is published in the [NHS Terms and Conditions of Service Handbook](https://www.nhsemployers.org/tchandbook). For the **current** rates, see our [Pay Rates](/pay) page.

### How the pay bands were restructured

#### From 1 April 2018

- Points were removed from the bottom of each pay band to start removing the overlaps between bands. For 2018/19 the bottom overlap point was removed from each band — the exception being band 2, where the bottom three points were removed. The restructuring of band 1 was complete from 1 April 2018.
- Band 1 closed to new starters from 1 December 2018.
- Further substantive pay points were removed on 1 April 2019 and 1 April 2020.
- A minimum rate of £17,460 was introduced from 1 April 2018.
- The top pay points in bands 2–8c were increased by 3%. The top points in bands 8d and 9 were increased by the monetary value of the increase to band 8c.

#### From 1 April 2019

- Further restructuring removed points from the bottom of bands 3–9.
- The top pay point in bands 2–8c increased by 1.7%; bands 8d and 9 increased by the monetary value of the band 8c increase.
- Staff on the top pay point of their band on 31 March 2019 received a one-off non-consolidated lump sum (1.1% of the top point for bands 2–8c; bands 8d and 9 matched the band 8c value).

#### From 1 April 2020

- Further restructuring completed the changes to bands 2–9.
- The top pay points in bands 2–8c increased by 1.67%; bands 8d and 9 increased by the monetary value of the band 8c increase.
- Staff on the following transitional pay points on 31 March 2021 moved to the next point up on 1 April 2021: £27,416 (Band 5), £33,779 (Band 6) and £41,723 (Band 7).
